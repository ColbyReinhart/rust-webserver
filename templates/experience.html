During my studies at Kent I participated in the Choose Ohio First Scholarship Program all four years. Choose Ohio First (COF) is a scholarship program which is offered to students in STEM majors. It's main two component include attending seminars related to emerging and enriching STEM topics and completing an annual team-based research project which is presented at a research symposium at the end of each school year. Sadly the 2020 research symposium was cancelled because of COVID, but my group presented and received the best presentation award for the years 2020-2021 and 2021-2022.

During my time at Kent State I also participated in HacKSU, which is a student-led computer science organization sponsored by the university which hold weekly lessons taught by volunteer students. Lesson topics include introductions to new languages and technologies, resume workshops/mock interviews, software application workshops, and general computer science lessons beyond the curriculum taught at Kent State.

Classes taken for my BS: Computer Science I-III, Discrete Structures, Statistics, Operating Systems, Computer Architecture, Structure of Programming Languages, Software Engineering, Algorithms, Introduction to Databases, Computer Communication Networks, Computer Network Security, Human Interface Computing, Applied Linear Algebra, Systems Programming, Computer Graphics, Intro to Game Programming, and a capstone software development project.
